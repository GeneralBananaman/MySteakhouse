A5 Wagyu Steak With Roasted Potatoes And a Wine Sauce

This recipe will help you craft an exquisite dish that highlights the luxurious richness of A5 Wagyu beef, paired with perfectly roasted potatoes and a luscious red wine sauce. The key to this meal is treating the Wagyu with care to preserve its delicate flavor while complementing it with well-balanced sides.

Ingredients:

For the A5 Wagyu Steak

  • 2 (6-8 oz) A5 Wagyu steaks, about 1-1.5 inches thick
  • Sea salt (preferably flaky) and freshly ground black pepper
  • A small amount of neutral oil (e.g., grapeseed or canola) for searing
  • 2 sprigs of fresh rosemary
  • 2 sprigs of fresh thyme
  • 2 cloves of garlic, smashed
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter

For the Roasted Potatoes:

  • 1 lb baby potatoes, halved
  • 3 tbsp duck fat (or olive oil if preferred)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Red Wine Sauce:

  • 1 cup red wine (Cabernet Sauvignon or Merlot work well)
  • 1/2 cup beef stock
  • 2 shallots, finely minced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 tsp balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

Prepare the Roasted Potatoes

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, toss the halved baby potatoes with the duck fat (or olive oil), minced garlic, rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper until evenly coated.
  3. Spread the potatoes in a single layer on a baking sheet, cut side down for maximum crispiness.
  4. Roast for 25-30 minutes or until the potatoes are golden brown and crispy on the outside, soft on the inside. Halfway through, give the pan a shake to ensure even cooking.
  5. Remove from the oven and set aside, keeping them warm while you prepare the steak and sauce.

Prepare the Red Wine Sauce

  1. In a medium saucepan over medium heat, add 1 tbsp of butter and sauté the minced shallots and garlic until soft and fragrant, about 2-3 minutes.
  2. Add the chopped rosemary and thyme and stir for another minute to release the aromas.
  3. Pour in the red wine and allow it to reduce by about half, which should take 6-8 minutes.
  4. Once reduced, add the beef stock and continue cooking until the sauce has thickened slightly, another 4-5 minutes.
  5. Stir in the balsamic vinegar for a touch of acidity, then remove from heat and whisk in the remaining tablespoon of butter for added richness. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  6. Keep the sauce warm while you cook the steaks.

Cook the A5 Wagyu Steak

Wagyu’s high-fat content means it requires minimal cooking—overcooking will melt away the prized marbling.

  1. Pat the steaks dry with paper towels to ensure a good sear.
  2. Season both sides lightly with sea salt and black pepper. Since A5 Wagyu is rich, a little seasoning goes a long way.
  3. Heat a cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat. Add a small amount of neutral oil—just enough to coat the bottom of the pan.
  4. When the skillet is hot and shimmering, carefully place the Wagyu steaks in the pan. Sear for 1-1.5 minutes per side, or until a golden-brown crust forms. A5 Wagyu cooks very quickly due to its fat content, so keep an eye on it. You want the internal temperature to reach 125°F (52°C) for medium-rare.
  5. In the last 30 seconds of cooking, add the butter, smashed garlic cloves, rosemary, and thyme to the pan. Tilt the skillet and spoon the melted butter and herbs over the steaks (a technique called basting) to infuse them with flavor.
  6. Rest the steaks: Remove the Wagyu from the skillet and let them rest for 5 minutes on a cutting board to allow the juices to redistribute.

Assemble and Serve

  1. Slice the A5 Wagyu steak into thin strips against the grain to preserve tenderness.
  2. Plate the roasted potatoes alongside the sliced Wagyu.
  3. Drizzle the red wine sauce generously over the steak, and garnish with fresh herbs if desired.
  4. Serve immediately, perhaps with a side of sautéed greens or a simple salad to balance the richness of the dish.

Tips:

  • Simplicity is key when preparing A5 Wagyu, as the marbling and flavor should be the star of the dish. Avoid heavy marinades or overpowering spices.
  • Serve with a bold red wine like Cabernet Sauvignon, Syrah, or Malbec to complement the richness of the beef and the red wine sauce.

This decadent combination of melt-in-your-mouth A5 Wagyu steak, crispy roasted potatoes, and savory red wine sauce is sure to impress any guest or elevate your own dining experience to new heights! Please check out our other recipes for more authentic flavors!
